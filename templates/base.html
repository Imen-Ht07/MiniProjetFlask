Project: Students' Adaptability Level in Online Education

Project Objective

The project aims to predict the level of adaptability of students in an online education environment using machine learning techniques. The main objective is to understand how different factors such as age, gender, type of institution, or financial conditions influence the ability of students to adapt effectively to online learning. These predictions can help educators, institutions, and policymakers improve pedagogical strategies and infrastructures to maximize the effectiveness of distance education.

Introduction

With the rise in popularity of online education, especially after the global pandemic, it becomes crucial to measure the effectiveness of this mode of learning. This project exploits a dataset including several characteristics influencing the level of adaptability of students, such as access to the Internet, the duration of online courses, or the type of device used. Through the analysis of these data and the application of machine learning models, this project seeks to identify the key factors that promote or hinder student adaptability in a virtual setting. The results obtained could provide useful information for designing more inclusive online educational systems that are adapted to the needs of students.
